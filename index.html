One pricing caution

Park Place is attractive and offers substantial care, but residents at Park Place and Ravines have recently faced unusually large fee increases during an ownership/financial transition. If touring, ask for the complete service schedule, all mandatory fees, the annual increase mechanism, and a three-year cost illustration in writing.

How to read the prices

“Published” means a current operator or 2026 directory displays that starting figure. “Planning range” is a deliberately broad estimate—not a quote—based on the published local examples in this list: assisted living starts at $5,067 at Ravines, City View lists from $5,470, and Stillwater memory care starts at $6,708. Premium suites, two-person transfers, continence care, medication administration, escorts and overnight checks can push totals well above the range.

Ask every residence for one all-in worksheet using the same care scenario. Otherwise a low base rent and a high-care package cannot be compared fairly.

Questions worth asking on every tour

  • How many registered staff and PSWs are physically in the building overnight?
  • Can she remain if she later needs a two-person transfer, mechanical lift, feeding help or palliative care?
  • Which services are included, bundled or charged per visit—and what changed in the last 12 months?
  • Can you provide the two most recent RHRA inspection reports and the current residents’ council contact?
  • Can we have lunch, visit again after 7 p.m., and try a respite stay before signing?
  • What happens if her needs exceed the licence or staffing model, and how much notice would the family receive?
